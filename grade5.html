Place Value in Large Numbers

Understanding place value helps us read, write, and work with large numbers up to eight digits (ten millions). Each position in a number has a specific value.

Place Value Chart (up to 8 digits)

Ten Millions Millions Hundred Thousands Ten Thousands Thousands Hundreds Tens Ones
2 5 4 7 3 8 6 4

Key Place Value Positions

  • Thousand: position 4 from right (thousands place)
  • Hundred: position 3 from right (hundreds place)
  • Ten: position 2 from right (tens place)
  • Tenth: first position after decimal point (0.1)
  • Hundredth: second position after decimal point (0.01)
  • Thousandth: third position after decimal point (0.001)

Types of Fractions

Proper Fractions

The numerator is smaller than the denominator (less than 1 whole).

Examples: 1/2, 3/4, 5/8, 7/10

Improper Fractions

The numerator is equal to or greater than the denominator (equal to or greater than 1 whole).

Examples: 5/4, 8/3, 9/9, 15/7

Mixed Numbers

A whole number combined with a proper fraction.

Examples: 2 1/3, 4 2/5, 1 3/4

Converting Between Improper Fractions and Mixed Numbers

Converting Improper Fractions to Mixed Numbers

  1. Divide the numerator by the denominator
  2. The quotient becomes the whole number
  3. The remainder becomes the new numerator
  4. Keep the same denominator

Example: Convert 17/5 to a Mixed Number

17 √∑ 5 = 3 remainder 2

So 17/5 = 3 2/5

Converting Mixed Numbers to Improper Fractions

  1. Multiply the whole number by the denominator
  2. Add the numerator to this product
  3. Write the sum over the original denominator

Example: Convert 4 3/7 to an Improper Fraction

(4 √ó 7) + 3 = 28 + 3 = 31

So 4 3/7 = 31/7

Adding and Subtracting Fractions

Same Denominators

When fractions have the same denominator, add or subtract the numerators and keep the same denominator.

Examples:

2/7 + 3/7 = 5/7

5/9 - 2/9 = 3/9 = 1/3

Different Denominators

When fractions have different denominators, you need to find a common denominator first.

  1. Find the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of the denominators
  2. Convert both fractions to equivalent fractions with the common denominator
  3. Add or subtract the numerators
  4. Simplify if possible

Example: 1/4 + 1/6

LCM of 4 and 6 = 12

1/4 = 3/12 and 1/6 = 2/12

3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12

Multiplying Fractions

To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together.

Example: 2/3 √ó 4/5

(2 √ó 4)/(3 √ó 5) = 8/15

Dividing Fractions

To divide fractions, multiply by the reciprocal (flip the second fraction).

Example: 3/4 √∑ 2/5

3/4 √ó 5/2 = 15/8 = 1 7/8

Adding and Subtracting Unlike Fractions

When fractions have different denominators, we need to rename them to have the same denominator before adding or subtracting.

Steps for Adding/Subtracting Unlike Fractions

  1. Find the Least Common Denominator (LCD)
  2. Rename both fractions to equivalent fractions with the LCD
  3. Add or subtract the numerators
  4. Keep the same denominator
  5. Simplify if possible

Example: 1/3 + 1/4

Step 1: Find LCD of 3 and 4 = 12

Step 2: 1/3 = 4/12 and 1/4 = 3/12

Step 3: 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

Answer: 7/12

Adding and Subtracting Mixed Numbers

With mixed numbers, you can add/subtract the whole numbers and fractions separately, or convert to improper fractions first.

Example: 2 1/3 + 1 1/4

Method 1 - Add separately:

Whole numbers: 2 + 1 = 3

Fractions: 1/3 + 1/4 = 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

Answer: 3 7/12

Subtraction with Renaming: 4 1/6 - 2 2/3

Step 1: Find LCD: 6

Step 2: 4 1/6 - 2 4/6

Step 3: Since 1/6 < 4/6, borrow from whole number

4 1/6 = 3 7/6

Step 4: 3 7/6 - 2 4/6 = 1 3/6 = 1 1/2

Multiplying Proper Fractions

To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together.

Example: 2/3 √ó 3/4

2 √ó 3 = 6 (numerators)

3 √ó 4 = 12 (denominators)

Answer: 6/12 = 1/2 (simplified)

Simplifying Before Multiplying

You can make multiplication easier by canceling common factors before multiplying.

Example: 4/9 √ó 3/8

Cancel: 4 and 8 have common factor 4

Cancel: 3 and 9 have common factor 3

Result: 1/3 √ó 1/2 = 1/6

Rounding Mixed Numbers

To round a mixed number to the nearest whole number, look at the fraction part.

Rounding Rules for Mixed Numbers

  • If the fraction is less than 1/2, round down
  • If the fraction is greater than or equal to 1/2, round up

Examples

5 2/7 ‚Üí 5 (because 2/7 < 1/2)

8 4/5 ‚Üí 9 (because 4/5 > 1/2)

3 1/2 ‚Üí 4 (because 1/2 = 1/2)

Comparing Fractions in Any Form

To compare fractions, you can convert to equivalent fractions with the same denominator, convert to decimals, or use cross multiplication.

Compare: 3/4 and 5/6

Method 1 - Common denominator:

3/4 = 9/12 and 5/6 = 10/12

Since 9/12 < 10/12, we have 3/4 < 5/6

Congruent and Similar Shapes
